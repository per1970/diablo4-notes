## Paragon Board Strategy — Following a Build Guide

### The Core Philosophy First

Board pathing now exists almost entirely to serve Glyph bonuses, Legendary nodes, and skill-tag synergies. Every point is either feeding a radius or it is wasted. That's the lens through which all decisions should be made. Don't think of the board as something to "fill" — think of it as a routing puzzle to reach high-value targets efficiently.

---

### Priority Order: What Actually Matters

**1. Glyph Sockets — your #1 priority on every board**

Glyph Sockets could very well be the most crucial feature in Diablo 4. These sockets, which can be leveled up, offer a staggering amount of damage or defense modifiers. Path to the Glyph socket first on each board, filling in nodes along the way that contribute stat requirements to the Glyph's radius bonus.

**2. Legendary Nodes — powerful, but don't be fooled by the orange glow**

Legendary nodes provide multiplicative damage, which makes them even more powerful than they may seem. If it fits really well with your build, then it should be factored heavily in your decision to use the board. However, Rare Nodes offer substantially greater and more diverse stats than other nodes on the board, making them especially powerful.

A common mistake beginners make: a lot of new players make the mistake of selecting their next Paragon Board solely based on the Legendary Node available, because the Rare Nodes inside often outshine this Legendary Node in many, many cases.

**3. Rare Nodes — massively underrated**

These are often what make or break whether a board is worth attaching at all. You can unlock a bonus when you reach a certain Attribute threshold — this threshold increases with each connected Paragon Board, making it more difficult to get that bonus. Prioritizing Rare Nodes early will ensure you get the bonuses for them.

---

### Rush Boards vs. Fill Boards — the Answer

**Rush to the next board. Do not fill.** The consensus among veteran players and top guides is:

Make a priority list of all eight boards. Pick the 5-6 best and order them from most desirable to least. Remember that you can pick a maximum of four additional boards in your final setup.

You path _efficiently through_ each board — hitting the Glyph socket and any Legendary/Rare nodes that your build uses — then move on. You are not trying to light up the whole grid. Most of the Normal (white) nodes in Season 13 are especially ignorable because raw stat growth has been stripped off the board and relocated to itemization, so those comforting 3% additive damage nodes you routinely path through to reach a Legendary socket are largely gone.

---

### Early vs. Late Game Setup

One pro tip from the guides: it may be best to set up two versions of your Paragon Board — one for early endgame and one for later when your build is fully online. Be careful not to over-prioritize Glyphs too early. They need to be found and leveled up before they reach their true potential.

In practice this means: early on, Legendary nodes can carry you while your Glyphs are still weak. Once you're farming the Pit and upgrading Glyphs (which in Season 13 now cap at **rank 50**), the Glyph radius becomes your dominant power source and you respec accordingly.

---

### The Practical Checklist

When following a build guide, do this on each board:

1. **Identify the Glyph socket** — path to it first, grabbing Attribute nodes that feed its radius bonus along the way
2. **Check if the Legendary node is part of your build** — if yes, route through it; if not, it may not be worth the detour
3. **Grab the Rare nodes your build uses** — these are often the real reason you're on that board
4. **Attach the next board ASAP** — don't fill dead space just to "complete" a board
5. **Audit whenever you change skills** — every time you change a skill's variant, audit your Paragon board for nodes that referenced the old tag, as Legendary Paragon nodes referencing specific skill categories are particularly vulnerable to this invalidation.

---

**Bottom line:** Rush to Glyph sockets → pick up Rare and Legendary nodes that your _specific build_ calls for → move to the next board. Filling boards completely is a trap that costs you boards and power you could've had earlier.
