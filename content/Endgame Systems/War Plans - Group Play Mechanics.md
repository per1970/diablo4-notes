# War Plans — Group Play Mechanics

## What is War Plans?

War Plans is a **permanent endgame feature** introduced in the Lord of Hatred expansion, launching alongside Season 13 on April 27, 2026. It is **not a seasonal mechanic** — it won't disappear after the season ends.

Instead of sticking with just one power-leveling route, War Plans lets players experience everything the endgame has to offer without making them feel like they're only slowing themselves down.

## How it works

When using War Plans, a player creates a playlist of up to **five activities** based on endgame modes:

- The Pit
- Infernal Hordes
- Helltide
- Nightmare Dungeons
- Lair Bosses
- Kurast Undercity
- Tree of Whispers

You queue them up at the **Command Table in Temis** (Skovos), then teleport directly to each in sequence. When all five are done, you claim a **War Chest** — a consolidated loot bonus on top of whatever dropped during the activities themselves.

As the Command Table levels up through repeated War Plan completions, the **quantity and quality of War Chest rewards increase**.

## Activity Skill Trees

Each activity has its own unique skill tree. As you complete activities in a War Plan, you earn experience towards their respective skill trees. These trees let you customize and modify how each activity plays — you can:

- Change the rewards
- Tweak the monster spawns
- Bring features from one activity into another

> [!important] Cross-activity benefit
> The Activity Perks you unlock from War Plans affect **every endgame activity** regardless of whether they're in a War Plan or not.

## ⚠️ Group Play — The Big Caveat at Launch

**War Plans runs are joinable in co-op. However, only the party leader receives full completion credit for the plan at the time of writing.** Non-leader players may not get progression counted. Blizzard is aware. Play with trusted people or solo until this is addressed.

In practice right now, if you and your friends jump into a War Plan together:

- **The party leader** gets full credit, War Chest, and Activity XP progression
- **Everyone else** participates and gets the normal per-activity loot drops — but their own War Plan progression likely won't advance properly

## Workaround for Groups

The community workaround is **rotating the leader role** — each player takes a turn hosting/leading a full War Plans run so everyone eventually gets their progression. It's tedious but works until Blizzard patches the credit system.

## Bottom Line

War Plans is excellent for solo play and will be great for groups once the credit bug is fixed. For now, if your group wants to progress efficiently:

- Rotate who leads each run
- Everyone benefits from loot during the run itself
- Only War Plan completion credit and Activity Tree XP are currently broken for non-leaders
