## Overview

Transfiguration is a Gear Modification recipe in the Horadric Cube that adds a free random power-up to a Legendary, Unique, or Mythic item. It is considered the **final step** in the crafting chain — do all other modifications first (masterwork, temper, socket, imprint) before Transfiguring, because the recipe has a very high chance to make the item **Unmodifiable** permanently.

Think of it as the spiritual successor to Sanctification from Season 11, but more controlled and without the mythic-tier effects.

---

## Recipe

| Input | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Legendary, Unique, or Mythic item | × 1 |
| Volatile Primordial Dust | × 1 |
| Tuning Prism *(optional)* | × 1 |

---

## Possible Outcomes (Non-Amulet)

The Cube rolls from a table of beneficial mods. Outcomes include:

- **Extra Greater Affix** — free bonus stats on top of existing affixes
- **Bonus flat stat** — additional stat line added to the item
- **Modifier types** — willpower, armor, crit, attack speed, main stat, elemental damage, resource generation, build-specific buffs (e.g. Melted Heart synergies)
- **Gem Strength** ⭐ — see section below; considered the best possible outcome by a significant margin

There is **one bad outcome**: Replace Affix — which can remove a valued affix (e.g. strips fire damage multiplier from a glove). This is rare, but worth knowing about.

### Multi-Transfiguration

On a lucky roll where the item does **not** become Unmodifiable, you can Transfigure again and potentially stack multiple mods. This is uncommon but possible.

---

## Gem Strength — The Best Outcome ⭐

Gem Strength multiplies the effectiveness of the gem socketed in that slot. It is considered the top-tier Transfiguration outcome and worth fishing for repeatedly.

**Why it's so strong:**
- On a **2-handed weapon**, Gem Strength is **doubled** — a Grand gem at 24% elemental damage becomes ~48%, and with Gem Strength applied you can reach 68%+ on that slot alone
- Across a full build this can amount to 120%+ extra elemental multiplier from a single transfigured slot
- Even on **armor pieces**, Gem Strength beats flat main stat rolls — e.g. ~700 main stat vs ~525 from a flat willpower outcome
- On **jewelry**, a resist gem (e.g. Ruby) with Gem Strength can push a single resistance to ~10,000, roughly doubling effective resistance on that element

**Priority order for Gem Strength farming:**
1. 2-handed weapon (highest value — doubled)
2. Jewelry (resistance or all-res gems become massive)
3. Armor slots (still beats flat stat in most cases)

**Practical note:** Gem Strength is one outcome among many, so expect to Transfigure repeatedly. Use non-GA tempers as an interim if you want to test runs without burning fully masterworked gear.

## Amulet Behaviour

Amulets work differently from all other slots. When Transfigured, an amulet receives an **extra Legendary aspect** rather than a generic mod.

- Legendary amulets can end up with **two** Legendary aspects
- Unique amulets receive **one** extra Legendary aspect
- The item **stays Modifiable** after a Kullean Transfiguration, so you can still make changes and even run a regular Transfiguration on top for an additional mod

---

## Kullean Tuning Prism (Amulets Only)

The Kullean Tuning Prism is the dedicated prism for amulet Transfiguration.

- **Effect:** Adds one extra Legendary aspect from a **utility aspect pool** (most classes have useful options — e.g. Rallying Reversal, Undying)
- **Item stays Modifiable** — guaranteed; no Unmodifiable risk
- **Re-rollable** — you can keep re-running the recipe to fish for the aspect you want
- **Tradeable** — the only Cube material that is not account-bound; drops more frequently at Torment IX+

### ⚠️ Known Bug (as of May 2026)

If you **imprint over a Kullean-transfigured Legendary amulet**, the extra Legendary aspect disappears permanently and cannot be re-added.

**Workaround:** Only use the Kullean Prism on **Unique amulets** for now, or ensure your imprint is fully finalised before applying the Kullean Transfiguration.

---

## Entropic Tuning Prism (All Other Slots)

- **Effect:** Flattens the outcome table — removes both the worst and the most powerful possible results
- **Guarantees Unmodifiable** — 100% chance, no exceptions
- **Verdict: Generally not recommended.** The only genuinely dangerous outcome without it (Replace Affix) is rare. By using Entropic you trade away the chance at top-tier outcomes like Gem Strength or crit/attack speed for marginal safety. Only consider this on a truly irreplaceable 4GA item with no backups.

## Crafting Order (Do Not Skip Steps)

1. Obtain a strong Rare or Ancestral base
2. Add / reroll affixes via Cube
3. Upgrade rarity (Rare → Legendary, etc.)
4. Imprint aspect at the Occultist — **finalise this before Transfiguring**
5. Add socket (Jeweler)
6. Temper (Blacksmith)
7. Masterwork (Blacksmith — fully)
8. **Transfigure** ← last step

> ⚠️ A common trap: Transfiguring an item while still on a low-range aspect roll, then finding you can't re-imprint. Lock in a good imprint first.

## Quick Tips

- Transfigure is **always the last step** — no exceptions
- On amulets: always use a **Kullean Prism**, and finalise your imprint first
- On all other slots: skip the Entropic Prism unless the item is genuinely irreplaceable
- **Gem Strength** is the top outcome to fish for, especially on 2H weapons
- Kullean Prisms are tradeable — buy them from other players if needed
- If the item stays Modifiable after Transfiguring, consider going again for a stacked mod
