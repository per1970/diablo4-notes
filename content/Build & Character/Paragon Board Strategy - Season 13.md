# Paragon Board Strategy — Season 13

## TL;DR

**Unlock more boards faster, focus on Legendary nodes and Glyph sockets. Don't fill boards.** Every point that isn't feeding a Glyph radius, sitting on the path to a Legendary, or unlocking a Rare bonus you actually qualify for is a wasted point.

## Core Priority Order

When following a build guide in S13, your point allocation should go:

1. **Path the shortest route to the Glyph socket** on each board
2. **Path to the Legendary node** (multiplicative damage — biggest single power spikes)
3. **Pick up high-value Rare nodes** along the way, especially ones whose attribute thresholds you can actually meet
4. **Move to the next Board Attachment Gate** as soon as the above is locked in
5. **Backfill Magic node clusters around your Glyph radius** only after you've laid out all your boards

> [!tip] Why Legendary nodes win
> Legendary nodes provide **multiplicative damage**, which makes them even more powerful than they may seem. That's why rushing them beats filling out a board with additive nodes — multiplicative scaling stacks differently and almost always wins.

## Why "Fill Each Board" Is the Wrong Instinct

Board pathing now exists almost entirely to serve **Glyph bonuses, Legendary nodes, and skill-tag synergies**. Every point is either feeding a radius or it is wasted.

If a Magic or Rare node isn't inside your active Glyph radius and isn't on your path to a Legendary or the next gate, it's dead weight. Veterans treat the board as a **road network connecting power nodes**, not a checklist.

## Season 13–Specific Changes

### Glyph upgrades moved to The Pit

Glyph upgrades are now tied to **Pit Tiers completed in the Artificer's Tower**, not to Nightmare Dungeon experience. If you have not unlocked The Pit by completing Rank II of the Season Challenges, you literally cannot improve a single Glyph.

### Glyph cap raised to 50

Up from previous seasons. More headroom but also more reason to focus.

### Concentrate Glyph Investment

Season 13 punishes spreading Glyphs thin. The Season Challenge objectives demand that **specific numbers of Glyphs hit specific rank thresholds**, meaning concentrated investment in **3–4 priority Glyphs** beats spreading 15 Glyphs to Rank 15 each.

### Legendary nodes are more conditional

Nodes that once provided unconditional bonuses now increasingly require specific conditions:

- Skill type active
- Enemy status matching
- Resource state

Plan your board around what your skills actually do, not what you wish they did. **If you swap a skill variant** (e.g., elemental conversion), audit your Paragon — you can invalidate your own multipliers.

### Free Paragon Points from the Season Rank track

Each **Capstone Rank awards 4 Paragon Points**. Bonus objectives through the track add more. Don't skip the seasonal challenges if you want to hit endgame benchmarks.

## Practical Pathing Example

For a typical S13 board you've just attached:

1. Enter at the gate, immediately path toward the Glyph socket (usually 5–7 nodes)
2. Slot the Glyph the build guide specifies
3. From the socket, path to the Legendary node — often on the opposite side
4. Along that route, pick up Rare nodes whose stat threshold you can hit
   - The bonus doubles the value when unlocked
   - When you attach multiple Paragon Boards, the thresholds for those Bonuses **increase**, making them harder to unlock — check the actual number, not just the node type
5. From the Legendary, head to the next exit gate
6. **Don't loop back to fill empty corners** unless your build guide explicitly calls those clusters out

## Veteran Caveat — Early vs. Late Board Sets

Legendary nodes might not be best for your final setup but are great at early endgame **before you've acquired all of your gear**. In these circumstances, it may be best to set up two versions of your Paragon Board:

- **Early endgame version** — heavy on accessible Legendary multipliers
- **Endgame version** — once your build is fully online with gear filling the additive damage gap

**Don't be afraid to respec.** Some Rare offensive nodes are gold while you're undergeared and additive damage is scarce, then become trash later when your gear provides that additive damage and you'd rather have a Legendary multiplier elsewhere.

Most good build guides include both a "leveling Paragon" and an "endgame Paragon" — check yours.
