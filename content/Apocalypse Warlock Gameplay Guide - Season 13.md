# Apocalypse Warlock – Gameplay Guide
*Sources: wudijo (YouTube), Maxroll.gg*

---

## Core Concept

This build uses Umbral Chains to rapidly build Annihilation stacks for Apocalypse, which circumvents the skill's long cooldown, unleashing a massive explosion every few seconds and evaporating entire screens at once. The gameplay is deliberate and rhythmic — you're a slow, heavy hitter, not a frantic button-masher.

---

## The Stack Mechanic (Most Critical Thing to Understand)

Every hit from a Hellfire skill generates 1 Annihilation stack, up to 100. Apocalypse becomes *usable* at 20 stacks, but the damage and AoE size scale all the way to 100 — casting early is a massive waste. **Always wait for 100.**

- **Single target:** ~4 Umbral Chains casts to reach 100
- **5+ targets in a pack:** as few as 2 chains to reach 100
- More enemies hit = stacks build faster — position chains through clusters

---

## Rotation

### General (Trash/Packs)

1. **Enter the area → immediately activate Valaok** (the floating goat). This costs 20 Dominance, loads your Overpower stacks, and grants a 500% Wrath regen burst for 6 seconds. **Do this first, every single run. Forgetting it is a near-total DPS wipeout.**
2. Spam **Umbral Chains** to build Annihilation stacks to 100.
3. Cast **Apocalypse** at 100 stacks → entire screen dies.
4. Repeat. The cooldown resets rapidly via Annihilation — you're back to stacking almost immediately.

### Boss Fights

1. Activate Valaok for Overpower stacks.
2. Cast **Doom** once to apply the **Hex** debuff — extends the Survivor Damage window from 2s to 6s, effectively doubling your burst window.
3. Ramp stacks with Umbral Chains (slower on single target — be patient).
4. Fire Apocalypse at 100 stacks.
5. Note: the Pyrosis paragon node gives a massive bonus only to *healthy* targets (above 50% HP). If you don't one-shot the boss, expect reduced damage after the first hit — this is normal, just keep cycling.

---

## Resource Management

Dominance is the only resource you actively manage. Casting Valaok to overfill Overpower also replenishes Wrath, sustaining your Umbral Chains spam. Umbral Chains only costs 35 Wrath — not demanding. Press Valaok again proactively **every ~20 seconds** or whenever Wrath runs low. Doom sits on the bar as an emergency Wrath restore, but you'll rarely need it.

---

## Sigil of Chaos — The Big Damage Amp

The Sigil of Chaos travels with you (attached via Hellfire Aura), meaning every Apocalypse cast is automatically fired from inside it. You don't need to manually place it. The kill count stacks passively through your casts.

Once you have the **Rite of the Nameless 5-piece bonus**, casting any Sigil skill recreates all of them at once — and since Hands of the Worldbreaker turns Apocalypse into a Sigil skill, it auto-refreshes your entire Sigil setup on every cast. At that point you can remove Sigil of Chaos from your bar entirely — it's fully automated.

---

## Overpower Stacks — Don't Ignore These

Valaok increases your maximum Overpower and allows you to overfill to 10 stacks. Combined with 70%+ Additive Overpower Damage per stack, this is a massive portion of your damage.

Stacks decay slowly thanks to the Chaos legendary node, but they *do* fall off. Build the muscle memory of hitting Valaok **every ~20 seconds**. If the stacks visually vanish briefly — don't panic, it's a tooltip bug, they're still active.

---

## Volatility & Self-Damage

The Ritualist Soul Shard with Scorching Fragment activates Volatility, keeping your skills permanently Empowered (glowing) — a significant additive damage multiplier. However, casting Hellfire Occult skills drains your life.

**You must have a healing source** — either the Undying aspect, life on hit on your weapon, or both. Without it, you'll kill yourself mid-combat. This is not optional.

---

## Survivability Reality Check

This build is strong but squishy. Your offense scales faster than your defense, so resist the urge to push higher difficulties than your gear supports. Prioritize survivability itemization early — the Warlock has minimal built-in defenses and must be geared accordingly.
